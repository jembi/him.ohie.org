The OHIE Facility Registry Community is a free and open initiative comprised of individuals, NGOs, and governments seeking to leverage facility registries to improve health outcomes in resource limited settings. A registry achieves this by providing a focused service to collect, curate, store and distribute standardized and current facility and resource data to the diverse network of eHealth and mHealth systems in a country.

The Facility Registry Community is equipped to provide field tested, open source, and scalable technology and implementation solutions to create a facility registry using local requirements and industry standards. These are formed out of field experience gained from implementations ongoing in countries such as Rwanda and Tanzania. Engage online with our facility registry community to learn more!

Facility Registry Implementation Guide

As a health leader in your country, it’s important to have solutions that are right for your local context.  We are committed to working closely with you and your teams to help implement a facility registry. A prospective implementation guide is available to help begin this process.

  • Identify
    Stakeholders
    and Motivations

    Identify Stakeholders and Motivations

    The implementation process begins by first identifying the current stakeholders and motivations driving interest in a health facility registry. This step is intended to answer the question: “Why do we want a new or different facility registry?”

  • Document Facility Registry Specifications and User Stories

    Document Facility Registry Specifications and User Stories

    A facility registry should be responsive to users’ needs and the local context. User stories and data specifications are two ways to describe: “What will the facility registry achieve and How should it operate?”

  • Carry Out Registry Specifications
    and Identify Gaps

    Carry Out Registry Specifications and Identify Gaps

    Using the specifications and user stories, create a viable initial version of the new or improved facility registry and in the process identify remaining gaps and requirements.

  • Iterative
    Development
    and User Testing

    Iterative Development and User Testing

    Using an agile and iterative approach has proven successful in the facility registry reference implementations. When the critical requirements have been completed, the next step can begin but the iterative process should continue.

  • Scale the Registry Implementation

    Scale the Registry Implementation

    To scale the facility registry, it is important to confirm the roles and responsibilities of stakeholders, train users and completing critical integrations between the facility registry and other systems.

  • Provide
    Ongoing Support

    Provide Ongoing Support

    Requirements and requests of the registry will evolve over time. Ongoing support is required to respond to the ongoing data, developer, integration, operations, and help desk requests from stakeholders.
